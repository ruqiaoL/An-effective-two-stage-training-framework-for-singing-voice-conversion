Audio samples for "AN EFFICIENT TWO-STAGE TRAINING FRAMEWORK FOR SINGING VOICE CONVERSION"

Abstract

The goal of singing voice conversion (SVC) is to convert the voice of a source singer into that of a target singer or speaker while preserving the content and melody of the song. Recently, many methods have been proposed to improve the performance of voice conversion by extracting speaker-invariant content representations. However, these existing methods either struggle to effectively preserve linguistic information or fail to adequately discard speaker information. In this paper, we propose an efficient two-stage training framework. In the first training stage, we designed an effective feature perturbation module that helps the content encoder to extract more effective speaker-invariant content features. Furthermore, a text-free second training stage is used to eliminate the mismatch between the first training stage and the inference stage. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms the mainstream methods for extracting speaker-invariant content features and significantly improves the performance of HiFi-SVC, the state-of-the-art SVC model.
